# D&D 5e Rules Reference

## Core Mechanics
- **Ability Check**: Roll d20 + ability modifier + proficiency (if proficient). DC set by DM.
- **Advantage/Disadvantage**: Roll 2d20, take higher (advantage) or lower (disadvantage). They cancel each other out.
- **Proficiency Bonus**: +2 at level 1, scales to +6 at level 17+. Added to attacks, saves, and skills you're proficient in.
- **Saving Throw**: d20 + ability modifier + proficiency (if proficient). Used to resist spells, traps, effects.

## Combat Basics
- **Initiative**: d20 + Dexterity modifier. Determines turn order.
- **Attack Roll**: d20 + ability modifier + proficiency bonus vs. target's AC.
- **Critical Hit**: Natural 20 on attack roll. Roll all damage dice twice.
- **Critical Miss**: Natural 1 on attack roll. Always misses regardless of modifiers.
- **Actions in Combat**: Action, Bonus Action, Movement, Reaction, Free Action (one per turn each).
- **Opportunity Attack**: Reaction when a hostile creature moves out of your reach without Disengaging.

## Common Actions
- **Attack**: Make one melee or ranged attack (more with Extra Attack feature).
- **Cast a Spell**: Cast a spell with a casting time of 1 action.
- **Dash**: Double your movement speed for the turn.
- **Disengage**: Your movement doesn't provoke opportunity attacks this turn.
- **Dodge**: Attacks against you have disadvantage; Dex saves have advantage.
- **Help**: Give an ally advantage on their next ability check or attack roll.
- **Hide**: Make a Stealth check to become hidden.
- **Ready**: Prepare an action to trigger on a specific condition (uses reaction).
- **Grapple**: Athletics check vs. target's Athletics or Acrobatics. Target's speed becomes 0.
- **Shove**: Athletics check vs. target's Athletics or Acrobatics. Push 5ft or knock prone.

## Conditions
- **Blinded**: Can't see. Auto-fail sight checks. Attacks have disadvantage; attacks against have advantage.
- **Charmed**: Can't attack the charmer. Charmer has advantage on social checks.
- **Deafened**: Can't hear. Auto-fail hearing checks.
- **Frightened**: Disadvantage on ability checks and attacks while source of fear is in sight. Can't willingly move closer.
- **Grappled**: Speed becomes 0. Ends if grappler is incapacitated or you're moved out of reach.
- **Incapacitated**: Can't take actions or reactions.
- **Invisible**: Impossible to see without magic. Attacks have advantage; attacks against have disadvantage.
- **Paralyzed**: Incapacitated, can't move or speak. Auto-fail Str/Dex saves. Attacks have advantage. Hits within 5ft are critical hits.
- **Petrified**: Turned to stone. Incapacitated, unaware of surroundings. Resistance to all damage. Immune to poison and disease.
- **Poisoned**: Disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks.
- **Prone**: Disadvantage on attacks. Melee attacks against have advantage; ranged attacks have disadvantage. Must spend half movement to stand up.
- **Restrained**: Speed 0. Attacks have disadvantage; attacks against have advantage. Disadvantage on Dex saves.
- **Stunned**: Incapacitated, can't move, can speak only falteringly. Auto-fail Str/Dex saves. Attacks against have advantage.
- **Unconscious**: Incapacitated, can't move or speak, unaware. Drop whatever you're holding, fall prone. Auto-fail Str/Dex saves. Attacks have advantage. Hits within 5ft are critical.
- **Exhaustion**: 6 levels. 1: Disadvantage on ability checks. 2: Speed halved. 3: Disadvantage on attacks/saves. 4: HP max halved. 5: Speed 0. 6: Death.

## Death & Healing
- **Dropping to 0 HP**: Fall unconscious and start making death saving throws.
- **Death Saving Throws**: d20 at start of each turn. 10+ = success, 9 or below = failure. 3 successes = stabilize. 3 failures = death. Natural 20 = regain 1 HP. Natural 1 = 2 failures.
- **Stabilizing**: A stable creature doesn't make death saves but remains unconscious. Regains 1 HP after 1d4 hours.
- **Healing**: Any healing on a creature at 0 HP brings them back to consciousness.
- **Instant Death**: If remaining damage after dropping to 0 equals or exceeds max HP, instant death.

## Resting
- **Short Rest**: At least 1 hour of downtime. Can spend Hit Dice to regain HP. Some class features recharge.
- **Long Rest**: At least 8 hours (6 sleeping, 2 light activity). Regain all HP. Regain spent Hit Dice up to half your total. Most class features and spell slots recharge. Only one per 24 hours.

## Spellcasting Basics
- **Spell Slots**: Expended to cast spells. Regained on long rest (short rest for Warlocks).
- **Cantrips**: Spells that can be cast at will without using a spell slot.
- **Concentration**: Some spells require concentration. Taking damage requires a Con save (DC 10 or half damage, whichever is higher). Only one concentration spell at a time.
- **Ritual Casting**: Some spells can be cast as rituals (takes 10 extra minutes, doesn't use a spell slot).
- **Spell Attack**: d20 + spellcasting ability modifier + proficiency bonus.
- **Spell Save DC**: 8 + spellcasting ability modifier + proficiency bonus.
- **Upcasting**: Some spells can be cast using a higher-level slot for increased effect.
- **Components**: Verbal (V), Somatic (S), Material (M). Silenced = no V. Restrained = no S. Focus or component pouch replaces most M.

## The Six Abilities
- **Strength**: Melee attacks, athletics, carrying capacity, breaking things.
- **Dexterity**: Ranged attacks, finesse weapons, AC, initiative, stealth, acrobatics.
- **Constitution**: Hit points, concentration saves, endurance.
- **Intelligence**: Arcana, history, investigation, nature, religion. Wizard spellcasting.
- **Wisdom**: Perception, insight, medicine, survival, animal handling. Cleric/Druid/Ranger spellcasting.
- **Charisma**: Persuasion, deception, intimidation, performance. Bard/Paladin/Sorcerer/Warlock spellcasting.

## Common Class Features
- **Barbarian**: Rage (advantage on Str checks/saves, bonus melee damage, resistance to bludgeoning/piercing/slashing), Reckless Attack, Unarmored Defense.
- **Bard**: Bardic Inspiration (bonus die to allies), Spellcasting, Jack of All Trades.
- **Cleric**: Spellcasting, Channel Divinity, Turn Undead, Divine Domain features.
- **Druid**: Spellcasting, Wild Shape (transform into beasts), Druid Circle features.
- **Fighter**: Fighting Style, Second Wind (heal as bonus action), Action Surge (extra action), Extra Attack.
- **Monk**: Martial Arts, Ki Points, Flurry of Blows, Patient Defense, Step of the Wind, Unarmored Defense/Movement.
- **Paladin**: Spellcasting, Divine Smite (extra radiant damage on hit), Lay on Hands, Aura of Protection.
- **Ranger**: Spellcasting, Favored Enemy, Natural Explorer, Fighting Style, Extra Attack.
- **Rogue**: Sneak Attack (extra damage with advantage or ally nearby), Cunning Action (dash/disengage/hide as bonus action), Evasion.
- **Sorcerer**: Spellcasting, Sorcery Points, Metamagic (modify spells).
- **Warlock**: Pact Magic (few slots, short rest recovery), Eldritch Invocations, Pact Boon.
- **Wizard**: Spellcasting, Arcane Recovery, Spellbook, Arcane Tradition features.
